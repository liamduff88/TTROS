## 1. Final recommended LinkedIn post

I spent the last few weeks rebuilding our internal AI operations system.
Here is the thing nobody tells you before you start.

Most small businesses do not need a giant AI transformation program.

They need one working system that does four things:

1. Removes the copy-paste hallway between tools
2. Captures the work the moment it shows up
3. Routes it to the right person or agent
4. Returns a receipt so someone can check it happened

That is the whole job.

The trap is architecture. It is tempting to build a bigger system: more
agents, more dashboards, more automation layers. None of that matters if
work still gets dropped between steps, or nobody can see where a task
actually is.

The lesson from rebuilding ours: useful AI operations are not about
adding more. They are about removing dropped handoffs, keeping the work
visible, and only spending model tokens on the parts that need judgment.
Everything else is plumbing.

If your setup feels more like a science project than a system, that is
usually why.

DM "SYSTEM" and I will tell you the one handoff I would fix first.

## Why this angle works commercially

This is a credibility play, not a pitch. Liam is showing the actual work
(rebuilding his own operations) instead of claiming expertise abstractly,
which reads as proof without needing a case study or a client name. The
frame (fewer dropped handoffs, not more architecture) is contrarian
enough to stop the scroll among operators who are already skeptical of
"AI transformation" language, and it pre-qualifies the audience: anyone
who DMs "SYSTEM" is already sold on the premise before the call happens.
The CTA is low-friction (a DM, not a form) and gives Liam a warm reason to
start a real conversation instead of a cold pitch.
