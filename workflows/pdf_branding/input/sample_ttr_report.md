# Time to Revenue — Sample AI Business Systems Report

Prepared for: Operating team  
Prepared by: Time to Revenue  
Report type: AI business systems assessment

## Executive Summary

The business does not need a larger AI roadmap before it needs cleaner revenue operations.
The fastest lift is available in the handoffs between lead capture, qualification, follow-up,
proposal creation, and owner visibility.

This report identifies three practical opportunities:

- Reduce manual re-entry across intake, CRM notes, and follow-up tasks.
- Tighten response time visibility so warm opportunities do not go quiet.
- Standardize the operating rhythm for AI-assisted reporting and next-step generation.

The recommendation is a focused workflow build, not a broad transformation program. The goal
is to remove avoidable delay from the path between qualified demand and booked revenue.

## Revenue Leakage

Revenue is leaking where the team already has buyer intent but lacks a reliable system for
turning that intent into timely action.

| Leakage point | Current risk | Business effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| New lead intake | Details arrive in scattered channels | Slow assignment and uneven first response |
| Discovery notes | Context stays in calls, chats, or inboxes | Follow-up quality depends on memory |
| Proposal follow-up | Next steps are tracked manually | Warm deals age without clear ownership |
| Weekly visibility | Reporting is assembled after the fact | Leadership sees lagging signals too late |

The issue is not effort. The issue is that too much effort is spent reconstructing what the
business already knows.

## Operational Friction

The current workflow creates friction in four places:

- **Capture:** useful client context is collected, but not always structured.
- **Routing:** qualified opportunities need clearer ownership and due dates.
- **Follow-up:** teams spend time drafting from scratch when the next action is obvious.
- **Reporting:** leadership review depends on manual summaries instead of live operating data.

These are good candidates for AI support because the work is repetitive, text-heavy, and tied
to clear business rules. They are poor candidates for vague automation because accuracy,
handoff clarity, and accountability matter.

## Recommended Fixes

1. Create a single structured intake format for qualified opportunities.
2. Generate first-draft follow-up notes from discovery context using approved templates.
3. Add a daily exception report for stalled high-intent opportunities.
4. Build a weekly revenue operations summary that separates pipeline signal from noise.
5. Keep human approval at the points where tone, pricing, or commitment risk matters.

The system should make the right action easier to take, while leaving commercial judgment
with the team.

## Implementation Plan

### Week 1: Workflow Definition

Map the current intake-to-follow-up path, define required fields, and agree on the minimum
useful operating report.

### Week 2: Local Prototype

Build the first version of the intake formatter, follow-up draft generator, and exception
report using sample data and approved language.

### Week 3: Controlled Pilot

Run the workflow with a small group, compare generated outputs against real team behavior,
and tune the prompts, fields, and escalation rules.

### Week 4: Operating Rhythm

Move the workflow into a weekly cadence with clear ownership, review points, and measured
business outcomes.

## Next Step

Start with a 60-minute workflow audit. The output should be a short implementation brief:
where revenue delay is happening, which data is required, which AI-assisted workflow should
ship first, and what result would justify expanding the system.

Time to Revenue should build the first workflow around one measurable commercial outcome,
then expand only after the operating team trusts the result.
