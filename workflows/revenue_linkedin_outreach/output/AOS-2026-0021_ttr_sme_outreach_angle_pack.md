## Best-fit customer

Time to Revenue is best suited to owner-led or operator-led SMEs with real demand already coming in, but too much revenue work still dependent on memory, manual admin, scattered tools, slow follow-up, or disconnected AI experiments. The strongest fit is a business that sells considered services, has enough lead or client volume for response quality to matter, and wants a controlled operating system for sales/service work rather than another generic AI tool.

## 5 practical outreach angles

### 1. Speed-to-lead is costing more than lead volume
Use when a business is already receiving enquiries through forms, calls, referrals, DMs, ads, or quote requests, but the response path is slow or inconsistent.

Angle:
Most SMEs do not have a lead-generation problem first. They have a time-to-response problem: the enquiry arrives, then the follow-up depends on someone seeing it, qualifying it, updating the CRM, and remembering the next step. TTR helps build the operating layer that makes that response faster and more reliable without removing human judgment.

Soft CTA:
Ask whether follow-up speed is already on their radar, or whether it is still being handled manually.

### 2. CRM exists, but the revenue process lives somewhere else
Use when the business has sales/admin complexity, multiple handoffs, or visible coordinator/reception/sales roles.

Angle:
A CRM only helps if the real work actually lands there. In many SMEs, the customer story is spread across inboxes, call notes, spreadsheets, calendars, WhatsApp, and memory. TTR helps turn that into a cleaner sales/service operating system: capture, next steps, reminders, handoffs, and practical AI support where it is safe.

Soft CTA:
Ask whether they are tightening CRM discipline, or whether the bigger issue is the handoff before and after the CRM.

### 3. Good team, too much manual admin around revenue
Use when the business appears busy, hiring support roles, booking appointments, handling quotes, or manually coordinating service delivery.

Angle:
The problem is not that the team is doing poor work. It is that too much of the repeatable work around leads, follow-up, scheduling, notes, and service handoffs still has to be pushed manually. TTR helps separate what should stay human from what can become a controlled workflow with approvals.

Soft CTA:
Ask whether reducing admin drag around sales/service is a current priority.

### 4. AI experiments are disconnected from actual workflow
Use when the business is trying AI tools, discussing automation, or likely has people experimenting individually without a shared operating model.

Angle:
Trying AI is easy. Making it useful inside the business is the harder part. The value comes when AI is connected to a real workflow: lead capture, qualification, follow-up, notes, task routing, approvals, and management visibility. TTR helps move from scattered AI experiments to controlled operating systems.

Soft CTA:
Ask whether they are still experimenting informally, or trying to operationalise AI safely.

### 5. No clear operating system for sales and service work
Use when the business has grown past founder-led memory but does not appear to have a clean rhythm for who does what, when, and where work is tracked.

Angle:
Many growing SMEs hit the same ceiling: more demand, more tools, more people, but no single operating system for sales/service work. Follow-up quality depends on habits rather than structure. TTR helps design the practical system: intake, ownership, next steps, approvals, reporting, and the right amount of automation.

Soft CTA:
Ask whether the current sales/service workflow feels clear enough for the next stage of growth.

## 5 discovery questions

1. Where do new enquiries, calls, referrals, or quote requests currently land first — and who owns the next step?
2. What parts of follow-up still rely on someone remembering to update the CRM, send the message, book the call, or chase the next action?
3. Where does customer or prospect context most often get lost: before the CRM, inside the CRM, after the first conversation, or during handoff to service delivery?
4. Are people already using AI tools in the business, and if so, are they connected to a controlled workflow or mostly individual experiments?
5. If you could make one sales/service workflow faster and cleaner in the next 30 days, what would have the biggest operational impact?
